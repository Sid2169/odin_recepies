Butter Naan

Butter naan is a popular Indian flatbread made with flour, yogurt, water, and salt. It is typically cooked in a tandoor oven, a cylindrical clay oven that reaches high temperatures. The naan is brushed with melted butter before serving, giving it a rich and buttery flavor.

Butter naan is thought to have originated in North India during the Mughal period. The Mughals were known for their love of food, and they often experimented with different flavors and ingredients. Butter naan is thought to be one of the many dishes that were created during this time.

Butter naan is a versatile bread that can be enjoyed with a variety of dishes, such as curries, stews, and lentil dishes. It is also a popular side dish for barbecues and potlucks.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/4 cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 tablespoon melted butter
  • Garlic, cilantro, and coriander powder (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the flour, sugar, and salt.
  2. Add the yogurt and warm water and mix until a dough forms.
  3. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5-7 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  5. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into 8 equal pieces
  6. Roll out each piece of dough into a 6-inch oval.
  7. Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium-high heat
  8. Brush each piece of dough with melted butter and place it in the skillet.
  9. Cook the naan for 1-2 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through.
  10. Brush the naan with more melted butter and sprinkle with garlic, cilantro, and coriander powder (optional).
  11. Serve the naan hot with your favorite curry or stew.
